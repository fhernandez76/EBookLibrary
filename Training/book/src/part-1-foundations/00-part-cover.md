# Part I

## Foundations

> *Three chapters establishing the project, the architectural model, and a
> compilable solution skeleton.*

This first part is the contract between the book and the reader. It
introduces the application you will build, the pattern you will build it
in, and the scaffolding you will hang it on. It does not yet write any
business code. By the end of Part I you will have a solution that
compiles, has its dependencies pointed in the right direction, and is
ready to receive the entities, handlers, and controllers of Parts II and
III.

If you are an experienced .NET engineer you may be tempted to skim
Chapter 2. Resist the temptation: the architectural decisions made there
shape every chapter that follows, and several of them are deliberately
non-default.
